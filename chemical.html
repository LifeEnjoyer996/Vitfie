The Chemical Engineering Students' Association aka Chesa is a student-run organization focused on supporting and promoting the interests of chemical engineering students. The club provides a platform for students to connect with each other, learn about the latest developments in their field, and gain practical experience through hands-on projects and competitions.

Chesa organizes a variety of events and activities to help its members develop their skills and knowledge. These may include guest speaker events, workshops, plant visits, and industry-focused projects. These events provide students with the opportunity to learn from experts in the field, network with their peers, and engage in hands-on projects.

The club also provides students with opportunities to get involved in research and development projects, allowing them to explore new technologies and applications in chemical engineering. Members can work together to develop new solutions to real-world problems, and contribute to the advancement of their field.

The leadership of Chesa is made up of elected student officers, who work together to plan and execute the club's events and activities. These officers are responsible for setting the club's goals, managing its finances, and recruiting new members.

Membership in Chesa is open to all students in the college who are interested in chemical engineering, and the club encourages students from a diverse range of backgrounds to get involved. The club provides a supportive and inclusive environment, where students can learn, collaborate, and grow together.

In conclusion, the Chemical Engineering Students' Association college "Chesa" is a dynamic and exciting organization that provides students with valuable opportunities to enhance their skills, knowledge, and employability in the field of chemical engineering.
