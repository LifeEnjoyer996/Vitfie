The Economics Society is a student-run club that focuses on the intersection of business, technology, and economics. This club provides a platform for students to explore the ways in which technology is transforming the field of economics and to learn about the latest developments in operational risk management, econometrics, and informatics.

The club's mission is to provide students with a broad and deep understanding of the economic and technological trends that are shaping the world today, and to help them develop the skills they need to succeed in a rapidly changing and increasingly data-driven economy.

To achieve this goal, the Economics Society offers a range of activities and events, including guest speaker series, workshops and training sessions, and case studies and research projects. Members of the club have the opportunity to learn from experienced professionals and academics, as well as to network with each other and with industry leaders.

The leadership of the Economics Society is made up of elected student officers, who are responsible for setting the club's goals, managing its finances, and recruiting new members. The club also has a faculty advisor, who provides support and guidance to the student leaders and helps to connect the club with academic and industry resources.

Membership in the Economics Society is open to all students who are interested in exploring the intersection of business, technology, and economics. The club welcomes students from a wide range of backgrounds and disciplines, including economics, business, computer science, and information technology.

In conclusion, the Economics Society is a dynamic and engaging club that provides students with a unique and valuable opportunity to explore the ways in which technology is transforming the field of economics and to develop the skills they need to succeed in a rapidly changing world.
