TRF (The Robotics Forum) is the official robotics club of Vishwakarma Institute of Technology (VIT), located in Pune, India. The club focuses on machine and electronics-based projects that involve creating robots and other robotic-related devices. TRF aims to foster a passion for robotics among its members and promote the application of technology in solving real-world problems.

TRF conducts various workshops and organizes sessions on robotics and electronics for students and other interested individuals. These workshops cover topics such as programming languages, microcontrollers, and sensor technology. These events provide an excellent opportunity for the club's members to learn new skills and share their knowledge with others.

The club's members have participated in several robotic festivals, including Robocon, one of the biggest robotic competitions in India. They have also competed in various state and national level competitions, winning accolades for their college. TRF's members have been recognized for their creativity, innovation, and technical expertise.

Apart from participating in competitions and festivals, TRF also conducts various projects that are aimed at solving real-world problems. For example, the club has created a robot that can be used in agriculture to help farmers increase their productivity. The robot can be used to sow seeds, spray pesticides, and even harvest crops.

The members of TRF are highly skilled and motivated individuals who are passionate about robotics and technology. The club's commitment to exploring new technologies and applications has made it one of the most prominent robotics clubs in the region. The club's focus on innovation, creativity, and problem-solving has inspired many students and professionals interested in the field of robotics.
