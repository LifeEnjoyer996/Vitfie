The Endurance Club is a college club that focuses on designing and building an all-weather, rugged, single-seat, off-road recreational vehicle intended for competing and racing at different levels. The club's primary goal is to provide students with an opportunity to apply their technical skills and classroom knowledge to real-world problems while competing in various off-road racing competitions.

The Endurance Club is typically composed of students from different fields, such as mechanical engineering, electrical engineering, computer science, and business. The members of the club work together to design, build, and test an off-road vehicle that can withstand the toughest terrains and conditions.

1. Design and build: The Endurance Club's main focus is to design and build an all-weather, rugged, single-seat, off-road recreational vehicle that can withstand various terrains and conditions. Members work together to design the vehicle's chassis, suspension, drivetrain, and electrical systems, among other components. They use the latest computer-aided design (CAD) tools and software to develop and refine the vehicle's design before starting the fabrication process.

2. Testing and optimization: Once the vehicle is built, the Endurance Club tests and optimizes its performance. Members use various testing methods, including dynamometer tests and track tests, to evaluate the vehicle's performance and identify areas for improvement. They also conduct experiments to optimize the vehicle's aerodynamics, suspension, and drivetrain to maximize its speed and maneuverability.

3. Competitions and races: The Endurance Club competes in various off-road racing competitions, including Baja SAE, Formula SAE, and SCORE International. These competitions involve racing the vehicle against other teams in various off-road terrains and conditions. The club's members must ensure that the vehicle meets the competition's regulations and guidelines and is capable of withstanding the race's demands.

4. Judging and parameters: The Endurance Club's vehicle is judged based on various parameters, including speed, acceleration, handling, and fuel efficiency. The club's members must ensure that the vehicle meets all the competition's requirements and regulations and can compete at its highest level.

5. Outreach and community engagement: The Endurance Club can engage with the broader community by organizing outreach events and activities. These events can include showcasing the vehicle and its capabilities to local schools, community centers, and businesses. The outreach and community engagement initiatives help to promote STEM education and make engineering more accessible to underrepresented communities.

In addition to these activities, the Endurance Club can also provide a supportive community for students who share an interest in off-road racing and engineering. Students can connect with like-minded peers, seek advice from senior students or faculty, and participate in activities that help to enhance their academic and professional growth.
